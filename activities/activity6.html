Activity 6: Boeing Graduate Program Information Session

Date: March 31, 2025

Time: 12:10 PM - 1:00 PM

Location: Conference Room 715

Category: Employer Events

Evidence: Attendance Certificate, Event Photos

Activity Description

Boeing Australia sent a recruiter to campus to present their graduate program opportunities. The session provided comprehensive information about the structure of their graduate program, application requirements, and key dates for the upcoming recruitment cycle.

The presentation covered:

  • Overview of Boeing Australia's operations and current projects
  • Detailed explanation of the graduate program structure, which involves rotations across different business sectors
  • Application timeline and critical dates for the upcoming intake
  • Selection criteria and assessment process
  • The recruiter's personal career journey at Boeing

What I Learned

The session provided valuable insights into Boeing's graduate recruitment:

  • Boeing's graduate program emphasizes cross-functional experience through multiple rotations
  • Computer science graduates are in demand for software engineering roles, particularly those with AI experience
  • The application process involves several stages, including online assessments, video interviews, and assessment centers
  • Boeing values both technical skills and interpersonal abilities
  • There are opportunities to work on cutting-edge aerospace technologies and defense projects

Relevance to My Professional Development

As a computer science student, the session was particularly relevant to my interests in software engineering and artificial intelligence applications. Learning that Boeing recruits from my discipline for work on advanced technological systems expanded my understanding of potential career paths.

The recruiter's emphasis on the rotational nature of the program highlighted the importance of adaptability and continuous learning in large corporations. This aligns well with my desire to gain diverse experiences early in my career rather than specializing immediately.
