Activity 7: Cultural Intelligence Program Workshop

Date: April 2, 2025

Time: 12:10 PM - 1:00 PM

Location: 323 Teaching Room

Category: Careers Workshop

Evidence: Attendance Certificate

Activity Description

This workshop was an introductory session to the university's Cultural Intelligence Program. The workshop provided participants with a comprehensive overview of cultural intelligence and its practical applications in professional settings.

The workshop covered several key areas:

  • Introduction to the concept of cultural intelligence and its four components: knowledge, strategy, motivation, and behavior
  • Interactive exercises to help participants recognize their own cultural frameworks and biases
  • Case studies demonstrating cultural misunderstandings in workplace scenarios
  • Practical strategies for improving cross-cultural communication
  • Overview of the complete Cultural Intelligence Program and its certification process

What I Learned

The workshop provided valuable insights into cultural intelligence:

  • Cultural intelligence goes beyond simple awareness to include adaptability and effective engagement
  • Understanding my own cultural lens is the first step toward appreciating others' perspectives
  • Cultural differences manifest in both visible (language, customs) and invisible (values, beliefs) ways
  • Effective cross-cultural communication requires active listening and suspension of judgment
  • Cultural intelligence is an increasingly valued skill in diverse workplace environments

Relevance to My Professional Development

As a future professional in the technology sector, which is increasingly global and diverse, this workshop provided essential skills for effective workplace interactions. Understanding how to navigate cultural differences will be particularly valuable when working in cross-functional and international teams.

The workshop helped me recognize some of my own cultural assumptions and biases that I had previously been unaware of. This self-awareness will help me become a more adaptable and empathetic colleague, particularly when collaborating with people from different backgrounds.

Additionally, developing cultural intelligence will be an asset in my career as it demonstrates to potential employers my ability to work effectively in diverse teams and global contexts. This skill set is increasingly sought after in the modern workplace where collaboration across cultural boundaries is commonplace.
